Abstract

What this project shows

Existing dynamic head-avatar methods often rely on fixed Gaussian–mesh bindings or black-box deformation models. TGMF instead introduces a locally continuous motion field that parameterizes Gaussian motion attributes with a second-order Taylor expansion, making local dynamics more compact, differentiable, and interpretable.

The method further combines local Gaussian motion with global head geometry through unified affine motion and bidirectional Gaussian–mesh optimization, improving temporal consistency while preserving flexibility in highly dynamic regions such as hair boundaries and facial contours.

Method

What to emphasize on the project page

  • Second-order Taylor motion for Gaussian position, scale, and rotation.
  • Interpretable motion coefficients corresponding to local velocity and acceleration.
  • Unified affine motion to couple translation, rotation, and scale consistently.
  • Gaussian-driven mesh coupling plus Differentiable Motion Backpropagation.
  • Smoother temporal transitions in mouth, eyelids, contours, and hair boundaries.
Presentation Guidance

Do / Not Do

Do

  • Lead with the video.
  • Show temporal stability, not only still frames.
  • Focus on expression transfer and local consistency.
  • State that the model is interpretable and local.

Not Do

  • Do not claim universal long-range dynamics.
  • Do not oversell high-frequency micro-motion fitting.
  • Do not hide the demo below technical sections.
  • Do not imply all future subject videos are already online.
